Temperature

Temperature: Temperature measurement has a rich history that includes multiple scales. The Celsius scale, named after Anders Celsius, was developed in the early 18th century. The Fahrenheit scale, created by Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit, emerged in the early 18th century as well. These scales were later aligned with the Kelvin scale, which is based on absolute zero and is now the standard for scientific temperature measurement. The concept of temperature measurement has continuously evolved over centuries.

Most Popular Unit:

Celsius:

Who: The Celsius temperature scale is named after Anders Celsius, a Swedish astronomer and physicist.

How: Celsius proposed a temperature scale based on 0°C as the freezing point of water and 100°C as the boiling point of water at standard atmospheric pressure.

When: The Celsius scale was introduced in the early 18th century, with the 100-degree range being divided into 100 equal parts.

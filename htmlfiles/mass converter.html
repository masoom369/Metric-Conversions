Mass

Mass: The history of mass measurement is intertwined with length and volume. Various cultures had their own systems for weighing objects. However, the modern concept of mass and the development of the kilogram as a standard unit evolved with the establishment of the metric system. The metric system's introduction in the late 18th century played a crucial role in the standardization of mass measurements.

Most Popular Unit:

Kilogram:

Who: The kilogram is part of the metric system, and its definition has evolved over time. Its initial definition was influenced by scientists like Antoine Lavoisier.

How: The kilogram was originally defined as the mass of one liter of water. However, the current definition is based on the mass of the International Prototype of the Kilogram, a platinum-iridium cylinder.

When: The metric system's development began in the late 18th century, and the kilogram's definition has undergone changes, with the most recent redefinition occurring in 2019.

Volume

Volume: Similar to length, volume measurement has ancient origins. The cubic cubit used by the ancient Egyptians and the amphora in ancient Greece are examples of early volume measurements. The concept of a liter, a metric unit of volume, was introduced in France in the late 18th century and became part of the metric system, with the cubic meter as its base unit. The metric system's adoption further standardized volume measurements.

Most Popular unit:

Cubic Meter:

Who: The cubic meter is part of the International System of Units (SI), which was developed by the General Conference on Weights and Measures (CGPM) and the International System of Units (SI).

How: The cubic meter is derived from the meter, with one cubic meter representing the volume of a cube with sides that are each one meter long.

When: The SI system, which includes the cubic meter, was officially established in 1960.

History

The history of measurement systems features John Wilkins, an English clergyman, and Gabriel Mouton, both making significant contributions. In 1668, Wilkins introduced a decimal-based universal measure, setting the stage for modern measurement systems. In 1670, Mouton proposed a decimal measurement system, marking the foundation of what we now call the metric system. This system, with units based on powers of ten, was further refined by French scientists. Ultimately, the metric system emerged as the global standard for measurements, underpinning science, industry, and everyday life. Wilkins and Mouton played key roles in the development of these standardized measurements, with Mouton's work recognized as a pivotal moment in the establishment of the metric system..
